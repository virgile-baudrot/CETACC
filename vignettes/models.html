Toxicokinetics model

Ingestion part

The dynamic of the internal concentration of the contaminant, also known as the toxicokinetics, may be describe by this simple equation:

dCj,indt=TrIngj,tot−kj,outCj,indCj,indt=TrIngj,tot−kj,outCj,in

dCa,indt=TrInga,tot−ka,outCa,indCa,indt=TrInga,tot−ka,outCa,in

where: - Cj,inCj,in and Ca,inCa,in are respectivelly the internal concentration for juveniles and adults - TrIngj,totTrIngj,tot and TrInga,totTrInga,tot are the trophical ingestion of contaminant for juveniles and adults respectivelly (see later for details) - kj,outkj,out and ka,outka,out the excretion rate of the contaminant for juveniles and adults respectivelly

Note that since TrInga,totTrInga,tot is constant, we have:

Ci,in=TrIngi,totkout(1−e−ki,outt)Ci,in=TrIngi,totkout(1−e−ki,outt)

A juvenile is only exposed to the contaminant through the maternal feeding (milk). Note that a new-born has likely been exposed through maternal gestation, that is, at time t=0t=0 (or birth date) Cj,in(t=birth)=Cj,init>0Cj,in(t=birth)=Cj,init>0.

TrIngj,tot=ηj×Imaternal×CmaternalTrIngj,tot=ηj×Imaternal×Cmaternal

And, for nn prey species, and adult is exposed to the contaminant through food items:

TrInga,tot=ηa∑i=1n×Ii×CiTrInga,tot=ηa∑i=1n×Ii×Ci

In both equations, we have: - ηjηj and ηaηa the assimilation rate in juveniles and adults, - IiIi: the ingestion rate of item ii (e.g., kg.day−1kg.day−1), - CiCi: the concentration in item ii (e.g., mg.kg−1mg.kg−1).

Ingestion rate:

Ii=ϕi×BiIi=ϕi×Bi

  • ϕiϕi is the proportion of item ii in the diet.
  • BiBi is the Biomass of item ii in the diet (e.g., mean biomass of whole or part of ingested individuals of species ii).
